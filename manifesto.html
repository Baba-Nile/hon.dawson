Hon. Dawson Mudenyo — Kwanza Constituency 2027

A People's Plan — Faith-Driven, Globally-Informed Leadership

This manifesto was built on years of listening — to farmers, mothers, youth, teachers, and elders across every ward of Kwanza. It is Dawson's contract with the people: to bring the same discipline, transparency, and strategic rigor he has applied advising NGOs worldwide directly to the challenges facing our constituency.

01

Healthcare For Every Ward

No family in Kwanza should lose a loved one to a preventable illness because the nearest dispensary lacks staff, equipment, or medicine. Dawson's NGO governance background means he knows how to push health budgets to actually reach the facilities that need them.

✓Lobby for fully-staffed, well-equipped dispensaries in every ward, with reliable drug supply chains
✓Push for a functioning ambulance and referral system linking ward-level facilities to Kitale County Hospital
✓Support maternal and child health programmes, including expanded antenatal outreach in remote areas
✓Champion NHIF/SHA enrollment drives so no household is one diagnosis away from poverty
"Health is not a privilege — it is the floor every other opportunity is built on." — Dawson Mudenyo
02

Education Without Brokers

Too many bright children in Kwanza miss out on bursaries because of favouritism, opaque criteria, or political brokering. Dawson has spent his career building transparent, auditable systems for NGOs — he will bring that same rigor to constituency bursary allocation.

✓Publish clear, needs-based bursary criteria and a transparent applicant tracking system every household can verify
✓Expand the Constituency Development Fund's education allocation, with quarterly public reporting
✓Partner with local schools to rehabilitate classrooms, labs, and sanitation blocks
✓Support TVET and skills training pathways for students who won't follow the university track
03

Clean Water For Every Home

Women and children across Kwanza still walk long distances daily for water that often isn't even safe to drink. This is solvable with the right strategic planning and sustained follow-through — exactly the kind of capacity development work Dawson has led internationally.

✓Drill and rehabilitate boreholes in underserved villages, prioritising areas with the longest walking distances
✓Expand piped water schemes from existing sources to more trading centres and schools
✓Establish community water management committees trained in sustainable upkeep
04

Roads & Infrastructure

Farmers lose harvests and traders lose customers when roads become impassable during the rains. Dawson will prioritise the routes that connect the most households to markets, schools, and health facilities.

✓Push for all-weather grading and graveling of priority feeder roads linking trading centres to the highway
✓Advocate for bridges and culverts at key flood-prone crossing points
✓Coordinate with county government on a transparent, ward-by-ward road maintenance schedule
05

Agriculture & Farmer Support

Kwanza's economy runs on maize, dairy, and horticulture — yet too many farmers face unfair pricing, poor storage, and limited access to credit. Dawson's strategic planning expertise will help build cooperatives and market linkages that put more money in farmers' pockets.

✓Strengthen farmer cooperatives for better bargaining power on input costs and produce prices
✓Advocate for affordable, timely fertiliser and certified seed access
✓Support construction of community grain storage to reduce post-harvest losses
06

Youth Employment

Kwanza's youth don't need handouts — they need real skills, capital access, and opportunity. Dawson has spent his career on exactly this kind of capacity development, and will bring proven frameworks home.

✓Partner with TVET institutions for trade and digital skills training tied to actual local job demand
✓Champion access to youth enterprise funds with simplified, transparent application processes
✓Support youth-led agribusiness and small enterprise incubation programmes
07

Women's Empowerment

Women carry much of Kwanza's economic and social weight, yet often have the least access to capital and leadership opportunities. Dawson will champion structural support, not token gestures.

✓Support table-banking and women's group access to affordable credit and market stalls
✓Champion women's representation in ward development committees and bursary boards
✓Back maternal health and family support programmes designed with women's input from the start
08

Community Security

A safe Kwanza is a Kwanza where businesses thrive and families sleep peacefully. Dawson will work with county and national security agencies to strengthen community-based safety, not just react after incidents.

✓Advocate for solar street lighting at key trading centres and junctions
✓Strengthen community policing structures and Nyumba Kumi coordination
✓Push for faster police response times through better resourced local posts
09

Transparent Governance

This is the pillar everything else depends on. Dawson's entire career has been advising organisations on exactly this — and he will hold his own office to the same standard he has demanded of others.

✓Hold quarterly public accountability forums in every ward, with published CDF and bursary reports
✓Establish an open-door constituency office with regular published office hours
✓Apply international governance best practice to constituency project oversight and procurement
"I have spent twenty years telling NGOs that good governance starts with transparency. I will not ask less of myself than I asked of them." — Dawson Mudenyo
🤝

This Is a Contract, Not a Slogan

Every pillar in this manifesto will be reported on publicly, every term. Dawson is not asking for blind trust — he is asking to be held to the same accountability standards he has built his career on.

Hold Me Accountable

This manifesto is a living document. Dawson will keep listening, keep refining, and keep being held accountable — long after votes are cast.
