A Note on This Page

These figures reflect community consultations and publicly available data, and will be refined as Dawson's team continues ward-level listening sessions. The goal is not to assign blame, but to build a transparent, evidence-based foundation for the manifesto — one residents can hold him accountable to.

Top Priority

Connecting Kwanza's Trading Centres to the Market

Farmers across Kwanza routinely report losing produce to spoilage when feeder roads become impassable during the rains. Traders describe customers who simply cannot reach trading centres for weeks at a time. A coordinated, transparent road rehabilitation plan — developed with the same strategic planning discipline Dawson has applied to NGO infrastructure projects internationally — is the single highest-leverage investment for the constituency's economy.

Sector-by-Sector

Where Kwanza Needs Investment

Urgent
Roads & Infrastructure

Feeder Roads Becoming Impassable in the Rains

Several trading centres become cut off from the highway during heavy rains, disrupting trade and access to emergency services.

Priority
Healthcare

Understaffed Dispensaries, Long Referral Times

Several ward-level health facilities lack adequate staffing and equipment, forcing long, costly referrals to Kitale.

Urgent
Clean Water

Long Walking Distances for Safe Water

Many households, especially women and children, still walk significant distances daily for water that often isn't safe to drink.

Priority
Education

Opaque Bursary Allocation Processes

Many eligible students and parents report unclear criteria and inconsistent communication around bursary allocation.

Ongoing Need
Agriculture & Economy

Limited Storage & Market Access for Farmers

Post-harvest losses remain high due to inadequate storage facilities and limited bargaining power with buyers.

Priority
Youth Employment

Limited Skills-Training Pathways for Youth

Many young people in Kwanza lack access to relevant vocational training tied to actual local job opportunities.

Naming the problem honestly is the first act of accountability. Solving it is the second.
